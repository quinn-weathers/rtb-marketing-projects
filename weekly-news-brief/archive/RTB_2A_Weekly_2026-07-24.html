Watch Federal Jul 20–24 · federal / NFA

The NFA deregulation offensive — ‘No Tax No Excuse,’ the SBR registry, and the suppressor tailwind

With the $200 NFA transfer tax zeroed out for suppressors and short-barreled rifles as of the year’s tax law, gun-rights groups spent the week pressing the follow-on argument that the registry itself is now vulnerable. SAF’s multi-court “No Tax No Excuse” filings and the NRA/FPC/SAF/ASA amicus in U.S. v. Machamer (6th Cir.) both frame the 1934 SBR registry as a “historical accident” left without a tax to justify it. Congress’s Hearing Protection Act (S. 218) is the legislative track for the same goal.

Moved State Jul 20–24 · state / courts

New Jersey — en banc Third Circuit strike-down, now with a July 31 effective date

The full Third Circuit struck down New Jersey’s AR-15 and magazine bans 10–5 (ANJRPC v. Attorney General, formerly Cheeseman) — the first federal appeals court ever to invalidate a state “assault weapon” or magazine ban, creating a direct split with the Seventh and Ninth Circuits weeks before the Supreme Court hears the AR-15 question. New Jersey State Police told dealers the ruling takes effect July 31. FPC (a prevailing party) and NSSF praised the decision; the state AG and Senate Democrats signaled they will seek to preserve enforcement.

Draft the “what changes July 31 in New Jersey” explainer now — comply-until-the-date discipline, what the injunction does and doesn’t cover, and the honest caveat that the state may seek further review. Keep both sides’ framing so the piece reads as a map, not a victory lap.

Moved State Jul 20–24 · state / courts

Virginia — the statewide injunction is live and AR-15 sales have resumed

Two outlets now confirm what was a forecast a week ago: under the Santolla v. Katz statewide injunction, no Virginia agency may enforce the “assault firearms” and magazine restrictions, and dealers (PSA among them) have resumed covered-rifle and magazine sales. The statute is enjoined, not repealed — the Commonwealth’s appeal, anchored by the Crump v. Katz “AR-15s aren’t protected arms” motion, remains the caveat on everything.

01cATF, courts & regulatory

The engine of the week: three circuits, a new DOJ front, and the fall SCOTUS anchor.
New Courts Jul 24 · courts / 1st Cir.

First Circuit upholds Massachusetts’ AR-15 ban — the split is now complete

A unanimous First Circuit panel in Recchia v. Campbell (decided 7/21) declined to revive a challenge to Massachusetts’ “assault-style” rifle ban, holding its 2024 Capen v. Campbell precedent controls and that Heller forecloses a right to arms “equivalent to those carried by the modern-day soldier.” It is the third federal appellate ruling on a state AWB this month — the Seventh upheld Illinois, the en banc Third struck New Jersey, and now the First upholds Massachusetts — producing exactly the entrenched split the Supreme Court takes up this fall.

RTB angle: This is the “why Viramontes matters” story in one sentence: three circuits, three answers, one month. Draft an explainer mapping the split for members — not as alarm, but as the reason the fall SCOTUS case is decisive. This frame carries the whole quarter of member content.

New Courts Jul 24 · courts / DOJ

DOJ opens a second front — moving to block the U.S. Virgin Islands’ Act 9113

On 7/22 the Justice Department asked a federal judge to block core provisions of the USVI’s new Act 9113 — a 15-round magazine cap and a bar on transferring firearms “manufactured to hold” more than 15 rounds — arguing the Third Circuit’s Cheeseman line already controls because the territory sits within the Third Circuit. Argument is set for August 19. It rides an existing DOJ suit and runs parallel to GOA’s nonresident-carry challenge (Henry v. Brooks) in the same territory.
