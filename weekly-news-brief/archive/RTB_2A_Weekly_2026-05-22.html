Christian v. James — 2nd Cir. strikes NY Vampire Rule (decided May 18; surfaced Thursday)

Most significant federal-circuit Second Amendment ruling since the VA signing. Judge Joseph Bianco (Trump appointee, 2019) wrote for a unanimous panel on the private-property-open-to-public question, joined by Judge Eunice Lee (Biden appointee, 2022); Judge Steven Menashi (Trump appointee, 2019) dissented on the public-parks question only (which the panel upheld 2–1). Plaintiffs SAF and FPC originally obtained the permanent injunction from US District Judge John Sinatra (Trump appointee, 2018) in October 2024. The ruling extends a near-unblemished post-Bruen winning streak against private-property carry presumptions, and lands while SCOTUS is actively considering Hawaii’s near-identical Vampire Rule under Wolford v. Lopez. Note: peaked at 84 on the single day of cycle coverage so far — will be a Week-in-Review centerpiece next week as the social/forum signal climbs.

Hot 1/5 days Peak 84 Avg 84 Week →

RTB angle: This is the most plug-and-play educational asset of the week. “What does the 2nd Cir. ruling change for a New York or Connecticut member today?” explainer should ship Friday/Tuesday after Memorial Day. Pair with Hawaii (Wolford) SCOTUS watch — same legal architecture.
