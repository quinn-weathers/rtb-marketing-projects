Top of mind · the week07 · 03

The week the summer’s 2A story went fully judicial and executive — and the movement’s signature question finally reached the merits. On June 30 the Supreme Court granted certiorari on the AR-15 “assault weapon” ban question (Viramontes v. Cook County and Grant v. Higgins, consolidated), the first time it has agreed to decide whether the Second Amendment protects AR-15-platform rifles.

The same order list held the magazine cases (Duncan, Gator’s) pending that outcome and denied the 18-to-20 petitions, leaving the young-adult circuit split intact. In the states, three laws hit their July 1 trigger: Virginia’s “assault firearms” ban took effect into a “muddled” enforcement picture (two case-specific injunctions plus an NRA-won statewide preliminary injunction that runs through Dec. 31), Virginia’s universal background checks returned, and Colorado’s SB25-003 came online. On the executive side, DOJ escalated its AR-15 enforcement campaign, suing Virginia as the third jurisdiction while its California “Glock ban” (AB 1127) posture stayed Unverified past the June 30 deadline. The empowerment read for members: a cert grant changes nothing for lawful owners today — it starts a case, argument is expected this fall, and a ruling is likely a year out. This week’s job is to explain what the law says now, what took effect July 1, and what is merely pending.

Virginia’s “assault firearms” ban took effect July 1 — into a “muddled” enforcement picture, not a clean statewide ban

Virginia’s SB749 “assault firearms” ban became effective July 1, but into what The Reload called a “muddled mess.” Two earlier injunctions (including Crump v. Katz) bind only their named parties, while the NRA secured a statewide preliminary injunction on June 29 against the assault-firearm and magazine bans that runs through Dec. 31. The Attorney General’s July 1 status letter and competing advocacy framings left retailers and owners reading carefully to see what is enforceable and where. Heat climbed steadily across the week (84→85→87→91) as the effective date arrived.

Moved SCOTUS · Cert granted Heat92▲ Jun 29 – Jul 2 · SCOTUS orders, SCOTUSblog, The Reload

RESOLVED: the Supreme Court GRANTED cert on the AR-15 ban question — Viramontes v. Cook County + Grant v. Higgins, consolidated

The story that anchored the entire week. The cert cluster read as Unverified on the June 29 order list, then confirmed June 30: the Court granted certiorari in Viramontes v. Cook County (No. 25-238; Cook County, Illinois’ ordinance) and Grant v. Higgins (No. 25-566; Connecticut’s statewide ban), consolidating them. Question presented: whether the Second and Fourteenth Amendments protect AR-15-platform and similar semi-automatic rifles. The Second Amendment Foundation is a plaintiff in both; every federal appeals court to review such a ban has upheld it. It follows Justice Kavanaugh’s 2025 statement the Court would reach the AR-15 issue “in the next Term or two.” Argument is expected this fall; a decision is likely by the term’s end (mid-2027). Heat held volcanic all week (94→89→88→95).

Hot 4/5 days Peak 95 Avg 92 Week ▲

This is a landmark for the movement, so calm, accurate framing beats hype and out-positions fear-led competitors. Build an evergreen case-tracker page you can update through argument and decision; it will earn links and repeat visits for a year.
